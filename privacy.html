Privacy Policy

How Spotify Stats handles your data.

Last updated: 22 June 2026

1. The short version

Spotify Stats is a static web application. It has no backend, no database, and no analytics. Your Spotify data is requested by your browser directly from Spotify's servers and never travels through ours.

2. Data we access from Spotify

When you sign in, you grant the app the following scopes:

  • user-top-read — to list your top artists and tracks
  • user-read-recently-played — to show your recent listens
  • user-read-currently-playing & user-read-playback-state — to display what you're currently playing
  • playlist-modify-private & playlist-modify-public — only used when you press "Sauvegarder en playlist"

The data we receive is rendered in your browser and never transmitted elsewhere.

3. Data stored on your device

We use your browser's localStorage to remember three things:

  • Your Spotify access & refresh tokens (so you don't have to log in every visit)
  • Your theme preference (light / dark)
  • The token expiry timestamp

You can wipe everything by clicking Déconnexion inside the app or by clearing your browser data.

4. Cookies, analytics, third parties

None. The app uses no cookies, no analytics service, no advertising, and no third-party trackers. The only external requests it makes are to accounts.spotify.com and api.spotify.com for auth and data, plus fonts.googleapis.com for the UI font.

5. Your rights

Because no personal data ever leaves your browser, there is nothing for us to delete on your behalf. To revoke the app's access entirely, go to spotify.com/account/apps and remove "Spotify Stats".

6. Contact

This is a personal side-project. For questions, open an issue at github.com/SamyNikaia/spotify-stats.
